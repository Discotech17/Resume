Hello! I included this snippet about me to give a better personal connection.

I joined the Air Force in 2013 in hopes to pursue a career in Pararescue(think combat medic on steroids). I was unable to get the job due to color blindness and received Aviation Resource Manager instead. My first base was Offutt AFB, NE where I worked with Airborne Linguists. I enjoyed my time there and learned how to navigate the Air Force as a young Airman. I exceled at my craft and was able to deploy twice out of Nebraska. My first deployment is where my programming adventure starts.

In 2015, during my first deployment, I started dabling with Python. At the time, I was unable to grasp the basic concepts to put even the simplest strings together. A couple other attempts at learning to program with nothing greater than failures, I gave up. Fast forward to Jan 2021, I decided to do a Udemy course for C#, and couldn't understand what I was attempting through the course. I stumbled across a Youtube vide about reprogramming your mind to think more like a programmer. All of a sudden, programming made a lot more sense.

I started with learning C# for the MTA 98-361 Software Development Fundamentals so I could attend Microsofts Software and Systems Academy. By the time I was ready to test, the course was full. So I followed some tutorials on Youtube, along with //inventwithpython.com/ tutorials. Being able to perform the actions and read a description of what they were doing, helped me develop a basic understanding along with some of my C# studying.
